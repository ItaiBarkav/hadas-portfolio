Overview This project was done as part of an operational command and control system used by the Israeli Navy. In order to protect classified navy information, some details have been altered in this case study.
The challenge Each position holder on a ship has a different responsibility in a threat situation. My goal was to improve how Navy ships access and analyze potential threats While addressing each user’s needs.
The story goes like this...
Oh no, There's a missile attack on the ship.
The controller's job is to group the same entities in the map, to have a clear view.
The controller groups again and again and again and again...
Now that the map is clear, other position holders can intercept the threat.
Research To understand the navy's users needs, I visited a ship and conducted interviews with each relevant position holder. My goals were to understand the surrounding environment of each position holder and their JTBD so that I could accommodate their different needs while making good use of the their desk space. Some of the questions I asked were: Which actions are taken in a threat situation? Which data is crucial to have? What difficulties are there in the process?
Personas Based on my research, I mapped 3 types of users, each one holds a different role in the situation.
Defence controller Responsible for alerting about potential threats and organizing the targets. Needs Fast tools to perform quickly with a focus on map interaction. Pain Point The map area can be difficult to navigate and use when it's crowded. Defence officer Needs Fast scanning of data: They need a single source that concentrates all relevant information. Pain Point Inconvenient consumption of data: the information is scattered in the map and not concentrated in 1 place. Commanding officer Needs Similar to defence officers. Pain Point Similar to defence officers.
Wireframing The Solutions In order to optimize information concentration, bring scanning speeds up, and reduce human error, I designed 2 solutions following these key principles:
Make actions as quick as possible Maintain contact with the map The side panel can’t hide the map
The Grouping Panel The problems I addressed were: Hard to group from the map: A controller has a difficult job when threats are close together. Manual grouping: This is the main task the controller has to do very quickly, and the current process takes 30 - 50 seconds. The solutions: Minimizing the map: Once the side panel opens, the map narrows down so that no content can be hidden. Grouping Panel: Suggestions - They save the controller time. Grouping from the panel takes 3-10 seconds. Alerts - Same as displayed in the dashboard, giving the controller access to everything they need in one place.
The Dashboard The problems I addressed were: Minimal area: The threat list area wasn’t large which made users have to constantly scroll to view all the threats on the list. Missing alerts: The old threat panel showed alerts on top of a threat, and since most of the time they had to scroll to view the entire threat list, incoming alerts were easy to miss. The solutions: Dashboard: I used a separate screen from the main system to display the dashboard. Now the users can view the map and their data both at the same time. Alert Area: The dashboard was divided into 4 sections. The right one provides space to alert users, and offer them choices about a given threat.
Usability Testing Goal To evaluate the usability of the features and measure how well they help users fulfill their role in a threat situation. Method 5 ship defense controllers were tested, whose performance was measured based on their completion times. Results Overall, the usability testing results were positive, with only one minor usability issue detected: Participants had difficulty distinguishing suggested groups from existing groups. The issue was fixed by providing different colors and visual indicators.
